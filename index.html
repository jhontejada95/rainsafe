The Problem

Traditional insurance
fails farmers.

A smallholder farmer in Colombia loses their harvest to drought. They file an insurance claim. Months later, after an adjuster visits, documents are reviewed and bureaucracy navigated, they might receive a partial payout — long after they needed it.

Most farmers don't even try. Insurance is expensive, complex, and designed for large agribusinesses. 500 million smallholder farmers worldwide have zero climate protection.

RainSafe changes this. If rainfall drops below 5mm in 7 days, the payout happens automatically. No humans in the loop.

Anti-Fraud System

Four layers of
protection.

C1
Parcel Deduplication on HCS
Each farm location is hashed to a ~111m grid cell and recorded on Hedera Consensus Service. No two policies can cover the same land. Permanently on-chain.
C2
72-Hour Cooldown Period
Coverage activates 72 hours after registration. Prevents farmers from signing up during an active drought event to immediately claim. Eliminates timing fraud entirely.
C3
GPS Photo Verification
Farmers must submit a photo from within 1km of the registered farm location. GPS metadata in the photo is cross-referenced against registered coordinates. Physical presence required.
C4
Coverage Area Limit
Maximum 500 HBAR coverage per registration. Prevents actors from insuring vast territories with a single smartphone, maintaining actuarial integrity of the pool.
